# YouTube Video Ideas

Goal: Drive traffic to the hosted tool site for AdSense revenue. Build an audience around the Hero Wars counter-hero problem leading up to and beyond the tool launch.

---

## Arc 1: The Problem (Videos 1–4)

**1. "I keep losing in Hero Wars Arena and I'm tired of guessing"**
- Show yourself getting crushed by an unfamiliar team with no idea what hero to swap in
- Pure relatable pain point — no tool yet, just the problem
- Targets: new/mid players frustrated by arena losses

**2. "How do top Hero Wars players pick counter heroes?"**
- Research and community deep dive
- Talk about how it's tribal knowledge, Discord guessing, no real resource
- Builds authority and captures search traffic from people already asking this

**3. "I tracked 100 Hero Wars arena battles to find patterns"**
- Manually record W/L vs. specific team comps in a spreadsheet
- Low-tech, but shows the idea forming
- Good "before the tool" anchor — honest and relatable

**4. "Why no one has built a Hero Wars counter tool yet"**
- Explain the game's complexity: hidden modifiers, skill interactions, stat thresholds
- Sets up why a tool is hard to build and why it matters

---

## Arc 2: The Build (Videos 5–7)

**5. "I'm building a Hero Wars counter tool — here's the plan"**
- Announce it publicly
- Show a rough wireframe or spreadsheet prototype
- Talk about using practice battles as the data source

**6. "Running 50 practice battles to train my Hero Wars counter tool"**
- Screen record yourself doing practice battles, explain what data you're capturing
- Raw footage with voiceover works fine here

**7. "First results: my Hero Wars counter tool actually works (sometimes)"**
- Show early, messy, honest results
- Imperfect demos perform well — viewers trust them more than polished fakes

---

## Arc 3: The Launch (Videos 8–10)

**8. "Hero Wars counter tool is live — try it free"**
- Full demo of the tool
- Link to the site in description

**9. "I used my counter tool for a full week in Grand Arena — here's what happened"**
- Results video: win rate before vs. after using the tool
- Best video for AdSense + watch time retention

**10. "The Hero Wars team compositions that beat everyone (according to 1,000 battles of data)"**
- Pure value and search-optimized
- Drives long-term organic traffic even after the launch excitement fades

---

## Ongoing Content (post-launch, keeps AdSense flowing)

**Meta/patch videos** (evergreen search traffic):
- "Best counter to [specific popular team] in Hero Wars"
- "Hero Wars grand arena meta this season"
- "Heroes to upgrade for arena — [month] tier list"

**Tool update videos:**
- "New feature: the tool now tracks guild war matchups"
- "I added 500 more battle samples — accuracy improved"

**Community content:**
- "You submitted your hardest teams — the tool takes the challenge"
- "Reddit's most-asked Hero Wars counter questions, answered by data"

## Screen Capture & Editing Stack (free/open source)

| Tool | Purpose |
|------|---------|
| [OBS Studio](https://obsproject.com) | Recording — game/window capture + mic + optional webcam overlay |
| [DaVinci Resolve](https://www.blackmagicdesign.com/products/davinciresolve) | Editing — cuts, titles, color; free tier is professional-grade |
| [Kdenlive](https://kdenlive.org) | Editing — fully open source alternative to DaVinci |
| [Audacity](https://www.audacityteam.org) | Audio cleanup — noise reduction, normalization |

**Workflow:** OBS records → DaVinci Resolve (or Kdenlive) edits → export MP4 → upload to YouTube

---

## AdSense Notes

- YouTube requires **1,000 subscribers + 4,000 watch hours** to unlock monetization
- Aim for **2 videos/month** consistently rather than bursting — the algorithm rewards cadence
- Titles and thumbnails matter more than production quality at small scale
- Also apply for **Google AdSense** on the hosted tool website separately from YouTube monetization
